# Conflict of Interest (COI): Health and nutrition

**Public Health Agencies:**
- **Includes:** CDC, FDA, NIH, WHO, EFSA (European Food Safety Authority), Health Canada, USDA
- **Stated interest:** Outwardly promote safety, healthy practices, healthy products, and the general reduction of illness.
- **Possible bias:** May obey those who fund them or their studies and may deprioritize subtle nutrient differences in favor of broad policy.
- **Impact:** High

**Big Pharma:**
- **Includes:** Merck, Pfizer, Johnson & Johnson, AbbVie, AstraZeneca, Roche Group, Novartis
- **Stated interest:** Promote pharmaceutical interventions, vaccines, and synthetic treatments to address health issues.
- **Possible bias:** May influence research and policy to favor drug-based solutions over preventive or natural alternatives, potentially suppressing data on non-pharmaceutical approaches.
- **Impact:** High

**Big Ag:**
- **Includes:** Bayer (Monsanto), Cargill, Archer Daniels Midland (ADM), Corteva Agriscience, Syngenta, BASF, JBS
- **Stated interest:** Advance large-scale agricultural practices, including use of GMOs, pesticides, and industrialized production; emphasize efficiency, yield, and food security.
- **Possible bias:** May downplay environmental, health, or sustainability concerns to maintain market control and subsidies.
- **Impact:** High

**Dairy Industry (Producers/Processors):**
- **Includes:** Dairy Farmers of America, Nestlé, Danone, Lactalis, Fonterra, Arla Foods, Saputo
- **Stated interest:** Protect consumer confidence in pasteurized products.
- **Possible bias:** Likely to downplay nutrient losses regarding processing or emphasize the “minimal impact” of industrial methods.
- **Impact:** High

**Supplement/Fortification Stakeholders:**
- **Includes:** DSM-Firmenich, Glanbia, Herbalife, Abbott Laboratories, GNC, NOW Foods, Pfizer (Consumer Healthcare/Centrum)
- **Stated interest:** Promote fortified products and focus on the stability of added nutrients.
- **Possible bias:** May frame negative outcomes favorably to protect market share of synthetic additives.
- **Impact:** Medium

**Raw-Milk Advocacy Groups:**
- **Includes:** Weston A. Price Foundation, Raw Milk Institute, A Campaign for Real Milk, Farm-to-Consumer Legal Defense Fund, Raw Farm USA, British Columbia Herdshare Association, Amos Miller Organic Farm
- **Stated interest:** Promote raw dairy consumption and advocate for policy changes legalizing sales.
- **Possible bias:** May overstate the nutrient benefits while downplaying the health risks/harms of bypassing pasteurization.
- **Impact:** Med-Variable

**Independent Researchers/Authors:**
- **Includes:** Gary Taubes, Nina Teicholz, Dr. Rhonda Patrick, Dr. Mark Hyman, Dr. Peter Attia, Chris Kresser, Dr. Casey Means
- **Stated interest:** Disseminate findings through publications and secure funding for ongoing work.
- **Possible bias:** Funding sources or personal branding goals may influence the framing and interpretation of data.
- **Impact:** Variable
